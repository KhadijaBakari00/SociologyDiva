A systematic, organized process that ensures objectivity and consistency in research.

Helps answer why events happen, not just what happens.

Steps of the Scientific Method

  1. Define the Problem ‚Äì Identify the research question.
  2. Review the Literature ‚Äì Study existing research.
  3. Formulate a Hypothesis ‚Äì A speculative statement about the relationship between variables.
  4. Select a Research Design ‚Äì Choose a method for collecting data.
  5. Carry Out the Research ‚Äì Gather data using surveys, experiments, or observations.
  6. Analyze and Interpret Data ‚Äì Examine results to confirm or reject the hypothesis.
  7. Report Findings ‚Äì Share conclusions in a report or journal.

üìå 2. Major Research Designs

1. Surveys

Definition: A method where researchers ask people questions.

Types:

  • Questionnaires: A list of written questions.
  • Interviews: Face-to-face, phone, or online discussions.
  • Opinion Polls: Used in politics and market research.

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Can reach many people.
  • Provides statistical data.

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • People may give socially desirable answers instead of honest ones.
  • Results can be biased depending on how questions are asked.

2. Experiments

Definition: Artificially created situations to test relationships between variables.

Example: A cancer drug trial where one group gets the drug (experimental group) and another doesn't (control group).

Hawthorne Effect:

Workers at a factory improved productivity just because they were being observed, not because of actual changes in lighting or working conditions.

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Researchers can control variables.

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • People may change behavior because they know they are being watched.

3. Observation

Definition: Watching people in their natural setting without influencing them.

Types:

  • Non-participant observation: Researcher observes but does not interact.
  • Participant observation: Researcher joins the group to study behavior.

Famous Example (William Whyte, 1930s):

Lived in an Italian immigrant neighborhood in Boston. Studied how gangs and social structures worked.

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Provides deep insights into social interactions.

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • Hard to remain objective when part of the group.
  • Small sample sizes, so results may not be generalizable.

4. Existing Sources (Secondary Data Analysis)

Using previously collected data like:

  • Census records
  • Medical and police reports
  • Newspaper articles

‚úÖ Advantages:

  • Cost-effective
  • No need to interact with subjects

‚ùå Disadvantages:

  • Data might be outdated or inaccurate.

üìå 3. Ethics in Research

Code of Ethics (ASA, 1997)

Researchers must:

  • ‚úî Maintain objectivity and integrity.
  • ‚úî Respect subjects' privacy and dignity.
  • ‚úî Protect subjects from harm.
  • ‚úî Preserve confidentiality.
  • ‚úî Disclose funding sources to avoid bias.

Ethical Issues in Research

Confidentiality Example:

A researcher, Rik Scarce, was jailed for refusing to reveal details about activists he studied.

Conflict of Interest Example:

Exxon Corporation paid social scientists to claim that jury verdicts were unfair after they were fined for an oil spill.

Multiple Choice Questions - Culture

1. What is the definition of culture in sociology?

A) The biological traits shared by humans

B) The learned and shared behaviors, beliefs, and values of a society

C) A type of government system

D) A set of legal rules

B) The learned and shared behaviors, beliefs, and values of a society
2. What are Cultural Universals?

A) Practices unique to each society

B) Practices and beliefs found in all societies

C) Modern cultural traditions

D) Historical customs that no longer exist

B) Practices and beliefs found in all societies
3. Which sociologist compiled a list of Cultural Universals?

A) Max Weber

B) Karl Marx

C) George Murdock

D) Emile Durkheim

C) George Murdock
4. What is ethnocentrism?

A) Understanding another culture from its own perspective

B) Viewing one's own culture as superior to others
