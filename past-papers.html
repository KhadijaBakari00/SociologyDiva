Questions 1-10

1. Alice, age seven, lives in a private home with her parents, her grandmother, and her aunt. Alice's family is an example of a(n)
b) extended family
2. Deviance is
b) behaviour that violates the standards of conduct or expectations of a group or society.
3. The marriage of a woman to more than one man at the same time is referred to as
b) polyandry
4. Lombroso claimed that:
c) hereditary problems cause crime
5. According to Functionalism, the family performs six functions. Which of the option given below is not a function of a family?
c) Transmission of social equality
6. In ......, the eldest male often uses the greatest power although wives are expected to be treated with respect and kindness.
a) patriarchal societies
7. Which one of the below is not one of the central concepts of Pierre Bourdieu's capital?
d) Romantic
8. Society brings about acceptance of basic norms through techniques and strategies for preventing deviant human behaviour. This process is termed......
d) social control
9. In some societies, many people are expected to marry within their racial, ethnic, or religious group, and are strongly discouraged or even prohibited from marrying outside the group. This is an example of a(n)
d) Endogamy
10. Which sociologist listed kinds of deviance as follows; innovation, ritualism, retreatism, rebellion?
a) Robert Merton

Questions 11-21

11. Interactionists are interested in how individuals interact with one another, whether they are cohabiting (live together) partners or long-time married couples.
a) True
12. Fifty years ago, a high school diploma was the minimum requirement for entry into the paid labour force of the United States. Today, a college diploma is virtually the bare minimum. This change reflects the process of.......
b) credentialism
13. If John attempts to use violence against his neighbour due to her ethnicity, it is ........
a) Hate crime
14. According to Functionalist, a criminal act can provoke a collective response that heightens group solidarity and clarifies social norms.
a) True
15. According to ......., there are five basic characteristics of bureaucracy, all of which are evident in the vast majority of schools, whether at the elementary, secondary, or even college level.
a) Max Weber
16. According to Jean Piaget's cognitive theory of development, children begin to use words and symbols to distinguish objects and ideas during which stage in the development of the thought process?
b) The preoperational stage
17. If we fail to respect norms, we may face punishment through formal and informal........
c) Sanction
18. According to ........ perspective, the reaction to the act determines deviance, not the act itself.
c) Labelling perspective
19. Students who graduate from high school without career plans but attend a college or university anyways, because that is what is expected. This is a/an example of........
d) ritualism
20. ........ is processes of socialization in which a person "rehearses" future occupations and social relationships.
b) Anticipatory Socialization
21. An unreliable generalization about all members of a group that does not recognize individual differences within the group refers to
d) Stereotype

Questions 22-31

22. Schooling refers to the informal process through which certain types of knowledge and skills are delivered.
B) False
23. The term ........ refers to the technology, organizations, and products involved with communication with large mass audiences without any face-to-face personal contact.
d) Mass media
24. A student may try to appear to be listening to the teacher more than he or she is if a teacher happens to be looking at him or her. This is an example for .........
a) impression management
25. ........ is a phenomenon in which the media provide such massive amounts of information that the audience becomes numb and fails to act on the information:
c) Narcotizing dysfunction
26. During the lockdown, where many schoolchildren were forced to continue their classes online, the lack of a stable connection and the appropriate devices meant some students were unable to keep up their schooling as normal. This is a/an example for .........
a) Digital Divide
27. Which of the following statements refers to the pessimistic view of media and their significance in contemporary society?
a) The lack of regulation and control
28. Which of the following theories is based on the assumption that the audience passively and directly accepts the message and does not critically engage with it anyway?
c) Hypodermic model
29. Studies of twins (Oskar Stohr and Jack Yule) raised apart suggest that both ........ and ........ influence human development.
a) heredity / environment
30. Who says that education plays an important role in the socialization of children because children gain an understanding of the common values in society?
d) Émile Durkheim
31. Which one is "false" about the main ideas of moral panics theory?
d) The media generally shows what really exists in the society

Questions 32-40

32. For Talcott Parsons, the central function of education is ......
b) to instil in pupils the value of individual achievement.
33. On the first day of basic training in the army, a recruit has his civilian clothes replaced by army "greens," has his hair shaved off, loses his privacy, and finds that he must use a communal bathroom. All these activities are part of ......
c) a degradation ceremony.
34. "A term used by sociologists to describe the willing exchange among adults of widely desired but illegal goods and services" refers to ......
d) Victimless crime
35. Kinship is culturally learned, however, and is not totally determined by biological or marital ties.
A) True
36. Functionalists emphasize that education has an important role in ...... For example, schoolchildren in the United States are hearing about the achievement of George Washington and Abraham Lincoln, British children are hearing about the clear contributions of Queen Elizabeth.
a) transmitting dominant culture
37. Honour, prestige, status are examples for ......
c) symbolic capital
38. "Same crime, same criminal history results in different sanctions depending on race, ethnicity, or social class" refers to ...... For example, a white CEO's decision that leads to large-scale environmental pollution wouldn't be «deviant».
a) differential justice
39. According to ......, childhood is a very new concept. It did not exist at all in the Medieval period.
b) Philippe Aries
40. According to ......, the goal of media research is to answer the following questions: Who says What, in Which channel, to Whom with What effect?
b) Harold Lasswell
