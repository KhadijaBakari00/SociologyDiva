Deviance

Understanding rule-breaking behavior ✨

📌 Key Concepts

Definitions

Norms: Rules for acceptable behavior in a society.

Values: Beliefs about what's right or wrong.

Deviance: Behavior violating norms (context-dependent).

Crime: Law-breaking behavior (a type of deviance).

Delinquency: Antisocial behavior by youth.

"Deviance is not a quality of the act the person commits, but rather a consequence of the application by others of rules and sanctions to an 'offender'." - Howard Becker

📌 Types of Social Control

Informal Social Control

  • Teasing
  • Embarrassment
  • Gossip
  • Social disapproval

Formal Social Control

  • Police enforcement
  • Court system
  • School rules
  • Workplace policies

📌 Sanctions

Positive Sanctions

  • Rewards
  • Praise
  • Promotions
  • Awards

Negative Sanctions

  • Punishment
  • Jail time
  • Fines
  • Social disapproval

📌 Functionalist View (Durkheim)

Deviance serves important functions in society:

  • Adaptive: Introduces innovation and change
  • Boundary maintenance: Clarifies social norms
  • Social cohesion: Unites people against deviants

Anomie (Durkheim)

A state of normlessness that occurs during periods of rapid social change or upheaval.

📌 Merton's Strain Theory

People adapt based on access to cultural goals and legitimate means:

Adaptation Cultural Goals Institutional Means
Conformity Accepts Accepts
Innovation Accepts Rejects
Ritualism Rejects Accepts
Retreatism Rejects Rejects
Rebellion Replaces Replaces

📌 Conflict Perspective

  • Laws benefit powerful groups in society
  • Differential justice: Unequal punishment by race/class
  • Richard Quinney: Crime is defined by those in power

📌 Labeling Theory (Becker)

  • No act is inherently deviant until society labels it so
  • Focuses on how agents of control (police, teachers) label behavior
  • Self-fulfilling prophecy: People may internalize deviant labels

📌 Types of Crime

Victimless Crimes

e.g., drug use, gambling between consenting adults

White-Collar Crimes

e.g., fraud, embezzlement, corporate crime

Organized Crime

e.g., mafia, drug cartels

Computer/Cybercrime

e.g., hacking, identity theft

Hate Crimes

e.g., racism, homophobia, religious intolerance

Professional Crime

Career criminals

Transnational Crime

e.g., human trafficking, international drug trade

📌 Additional Concepts

Moral Panic

Widespread fear of deviance often exaggerated by media

Recidivism

Tendency of convicted criminals to reoffend

Differential Association Theory (Sutherland)

Deviance is learned through social interactions
